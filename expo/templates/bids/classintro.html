Class Application

Thank you for your interest in contributing to The Great Burlesque Expo 2014.

GBE is February 7-9, 2014 at The Hyatt Regency Hotel in Cambridge, MA.

GBE is looking to find interesting classes, panels and other convention presentations that satisfy as many different attendees as we can. We are looking for dance classes, circus arts classes, other movement classes, stagecraft classes, performing arts business management classes, panels and discussions, and other material that may be of interest to amateur or professional burlesque performer.

We encourage both experienced and new teachers to bid events. Whether it's a new idea or material that's been run before, we're interested in what you have to propose.

The Great Burlesque Expo 2014 actively solicits bids in the months leading up to the Expo. As the attendance grows, based on registration numbers, we may need a additional round of bids to fill in the slate of games already accepted for the convention.

Please fill out the submission form with as much information as possible. This is the best way to help us evaluate your class or panel quickly!

Class Proposal Instructions

Classes at the Expo include Lectures, Movement Classes, and Workshops class formats. If you want to propose a Panel, please click here.

A Lecture is a class taught by one or two people in which the information flows primarily from the instructor(s) to the students. They can be intellectual or physical. An example of a lecture might be "The History of Burlesque", "Taxes for Performers", "Marketing Yourself and Your Work", and "Audio Editing 101". The room will be set in a classic class room style, with long tables, chairs, and a desk and chair for the presenter.

Movement Classes are physically active classes taught in a wide open space. They can be physical skill, including dance, circus arts, theater or something completely different. Examples include - "Chair Dance Basics", "The Arms and Hands of a Dancer" and "Limber Up! Improving Flexibility". The space will be a private, open space. See the choices below to describe your flooring needs.

A Workshop is a hands-on, practical, class, usually two-hours long. Participants in workshops learn a skill or craft that is taught through doing. Workshops often have a materials fee associated with them. Examples of a workshop might be "Making a Tiny Little Top Hat" or "How to Decorate a Bra". The room setup will accommodate hands on work with tables and chairs arranged in a way that facilitates instructor led hands on work, and basic sewing supplies can be provided (scissors, basic thread, pins, needs, glue) - instructors are expected to bring any materials that are consumed during the course of the class. Please include any tools not listed above if they cannot be provided by the instructor.
